About

Six years. Five banks. One job.

Make complex software feel like it was built for the people using it. That is what I do at every company I join.

I'm Chris, a UX designer in the NYC area who makes complex systems feel simple, and saves businesses money doing it.

I've always been curious about how things work, and that curiosity is what pulled me into design. While studying at university I worked part-time at TD Bank, where I saw first-hand how tangled financial workflows can get. That sent me exploring across tech, from software engineering to UX design, until I found where I fit.

For the past 6 years I've worked with global banks and startups as a consultant, combining UX design with project management. That mix lets me design products that are both usable and buildable, because I understand what it takes to actually ship.

I run the full process: research, synthesis, prototyping, and delivery. I keep business goals and user needs aligned the whole way, and I've done it remote, hybrid, and under tight deadlines with real regulatory constraints.

"My work matters most when clarity is needed in complexity."
