Work Manager Hub

One hub for 1,000+ analysts.

Consolidated seven disconnected tools across four regions at a global bank.

Role
UX Designer
+ Researcher
Company
Global Corporate Bank
Timeline
18 months
Users Impacted
1,000+ across 4 regions
TLDR

A global bank had 7+ disconnected tools, no shared visibility, and teams unknowingly duplicating each other's work across four regions. No internal precedent existed. I led the research and design of a hub built entirely from scratch, and it shipped to 1,000+ users with measurable impact from day one.

The Challenge

A global bank. Dozens of tools. No way to see the whole picture.

Global teams were paralyzed by disconnected tools, forcing employees to switch systems dozens of times a day. The task: consolidate critical workflows into a single workspace while maintaining compliance, scalability, and role-based personalization.

01

Tool fragmentation at scale

Employees used 7+ disconnected tools daily, switching context 40+ times per day with no single source of truth.

02

No role-based prioritization

Urgent tasks were buried alongside routine work. Managers had no visibility into blockers across their teams.

03

Duplicate work across regions

75% of teams reported unknowingly duplicating work. Missed dependencies caused deadline slips globally.

04

Zero starting point

No internal precedent. No existing tool to evolve. Designed entirely from scratch.

Plan of Action

With no precedent to lean on, I focused on three things at once.

Designing from scratch on a live deadline meant I couldn't research, then design, then validate in sequence. I ran three tracks in parallel, so every design decision was grounded in a real user need and pressure-tested before it shipped.

01

Understand the real workflows

Before consolidating anything, I needed to know how work actually moved across 7+ tools and four regions. Assumptions were the thing that had failed similar efforts elsewhere.

02

Design for role, not for everyone

A junior analyst and a regional manager needed completely different views. I planned the system around role-based prioritization from the first wireframe, not as a later add-on.

03

Build adoption into the design

A tool nobody adopts is a failed consolidation. I treated the rollout, training, and measurement as part of the design problem, planned from day one rather than bolted on at launch.

Research

30+ conversations across 4 regions. Three things kept coming up.

I spoke directly to daily users at every level, from junior staff to senior managers, studying habits, mapping workflows, and running A/B tests before committing to build.

Swimlane mapping exposed overlapping workflows, bottlenecks, and integration points across 7+ legacy tools. Each region had developed its own workarounds.
01

Tool Fragmentation

Users juggled 5+ tools daily. Some tasks required 2+ tools simultaneously. No central source of truth.

02

Buried Priorities

60% of tasks lacked clear priority signals. Urgent items mixed with routine work. No role-specific filtering.

03

Visibility Gap

75% of teams reported duplicate work. No real-time status updates across global regions.

Key insight: The friction wasn't tool overload, it was fragmented priorities. Without understanding the interdependencies between tools and roles, the hub risked becoming just another tool to manage, not a productivity multiplier.

I narrowed MVP scope to the highest-impact workflows, leaving low-impact integrations for a later release.

Design

Built for global scale, role-aware from day one, AI-ready for what's next.

High and low-fidelity wireframes and prototypes tailored to role-based dashboards. Partnered with developers for design system compliance and accessibility. Data structured in the UI to support future AI features.

Low-fidelity prototypes validated layout, data hierarchy, and role-based workflows before committing development resources.
High-fidelity designs refined into a role-aware dashboard with a priority panel, real-time team feed, and AI-ready smart filters.

Results

Shipped on time. Adopted fast. Measurable from day one.

Shipping wasn't the finish line, adoption was. I paired the launch with a global training program, manuals, live sessions, 1:1 coaching, and an online Q&A channel, then tracked adoption and time savings on dashboards shared with leadership.

Rollout workflow diagram bridged the gap between abstract features and day-to-day tasks, reducing onboarding time and ensuring consistent adoption across regions.

Trade-off: Staged rollout by region to maximize adoption over speed. Longer timeline, but every team had the support they needed to transition effectively.

The Work Manager Hub is now a core tool used daily across global teams. It became the single source of truth, paving the way for AI-driven predictive tasking in future releases.
